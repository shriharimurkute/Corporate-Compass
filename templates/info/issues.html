Common Issues Faced by Municipalities & Citizens

This page outlines some of the common civic problems that citizens encounter and which typically fall under the responsibility of the local municipal body. Corporate Compass aims to help address these.

Waste Management

Issues related to irregular garbage collection, overflowing bins, lack of segregation, dumping in open spaces, and inefficient disposal methods.

Water Supply & Sewerage

Problems like inadequate water supply, contaminated water, pipe leakages, blocked drains, overflowing sewers, and lack of proper drainage systems leading to waterlogging.

Roads & Footpaths

Complaints about potholes, poorly maintained roads, broken or encroached footpaths, lack of ramps for accessibility, and unsafe road conditions.

Street Lighting

Issues concerning non-functional streetlights, inadequate lighting in areas leading to safety concerns, or lights remaining on during the day.

Unauthorized Constructions & Encroachments

Complaints regarding illegal constructions, deviations from approved plans, and encroachment on public land, footpaths, or roads.

Parks & Public Spaces

Issues related to poor maintenance of parks and playgrounds, lack of facilities, broken equipment, or misuse of public spaces.

Public Nuisance

Complaints about noise pollution (beyond permissible limits), stray animal menace (dogs, cattle), or other activities causing public inconvenience that fall under municipal regulation.

Public Health & Sanitation

Concerns about mosquito breeding grounds, unhygienic conditions in public areas, lack of public toilets, or issues related to food safety inspections (if handled by municipality).

If you are facing issues like these, please use the Submit Complaint feature to register your grievance with the appropriate agency department.
