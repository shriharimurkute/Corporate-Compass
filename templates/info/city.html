About Belgaum (Belagavi)

General information and key facts about Belgaum (Belagavi).

Overview

Belgaum, officially known as **Belagavi**, is a significant city located in the northwestern part of the state of Karnataka, India. Situated in the Western Ghats near the borders of Maharashtra and Goa, it boasts a rich historical and cultural heritage. Known for its strategic military importance (housing the Maratha Light Infantry Regimental Centre - MLIRC) and pleasant climate, it serves as the administrative headquarters of the Belagavi District. It's also a key educational and industrial hub, particularly known for foundries and heavy machinery. The **Suvarna Vidhana Soudha**, a secondary legislature building for Karnataka, is located here.

Demographics (Approx. 2011 Census)
  • Population (City): Approx. 490,045
  • Population (Urban Agglomeration): Approx. 610,350
  • Area (City Corporation): Approx. 94 sq. km
  • Literacy Rate: Approx. 89.82%
  • Key Languages: Kannada (Official), Marathi (Widely Spoken), Hindi, Urdu, English

Data is based on the 2011 Census and may have changed. Refer to official sources for the latest figures.

Local Governance

The city is governed by the Belagavi City Corporation (BCC). Key responsibilities include civic amenities, infrastructure development, waste management, and local planning.

The city is divided into 58 administrative wards.

Key Landmarks & Attractions
  • Belgaum Fort: A historic fort with diverse architectural influences and temples/mosques inside.
  • Kamal Basadi: A notable Jain temple within the fort complex.
  • Kapileshwar Temple: An ancient Shiva temple, often called the "Dakshina Kashi".
  • Suvarna Vidhana Soudha: The impressive second legislature building of Karnataka.
  • Military Mahadeva Temple: A unique and well-maintained temple within the military area.
  • St. Mary's Church: An example of Gothic architecture.
  • Gokak Falls (Nearby): A scenic waterfall often visited from Belgaum.
  • Kittur Fort (Nearby): Historically significant fort associated with Rani Chennamma.
